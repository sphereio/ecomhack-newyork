About

Back in 1996, we were excited when we could only find a product in a web store, put it into the cart and have it delivered to our doorsteps. A few years forward, customer reviews and social sharing features made shopping already a lot more fun. Entering the Web 3.0 era, commerce is increasingly melting with content, community and context to create never seen before personal shopping experiences.

With the help of new technology, merchants and brands can analyze their customers’ characteristics and behaviour to engage with them within the context of their needs, desires, preferences, timing and location, to surprise and delight them and build long-lasting customer relationships.

The upcoming ecomhack New York is all about how smartphones, wearables, beacons and other emerging devices enable creating these personal experiences that make shopping more individual, more efficient – and more fun.

Rules

1. Start your project from scratch at the first day of the event.

2. Work on an idea related to the topic "Personal Commerce".

3. Include commercetools and any other of the participating APIs in your concept.

4. Team size 1 - 5 people.

5. Powerpoint-free zone: It’s your demo that’s relevant - If it’s not fully functional (yet), just walk us through the code.
